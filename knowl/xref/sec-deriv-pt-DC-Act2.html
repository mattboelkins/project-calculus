Activity 1.3.5.
Let \(g(x) = |x|\text{.}\) Recall that if \(x \ge 0\text{,}\) then \(|x| = x\text{,}\) and if \(x \lt 0\text{,}\) then \(|x| = -x\text{.}\)
  1. Determine \(g'(3)\) and \(g'(5)\text{.}\)
  2. Determine \(g'(-2)\) and \(g'(-6)\text{.}\)
  3. For \(g(x) = |x|\text{,}\) the limit definition of the derivative shows that
    \begin{equation*} g'(0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{g(0+h) - g(0)}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{|0+h| - |0|}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{|h|}{h}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    If \(h\) is a small positive number, what is the value of \(\frac{|h|}{h}\text{?}\) If \(h\) is a small negative number, what is the value of \(\frac{|h|}{h}\text{?}\)
  4. Explain why \(\displaystyle \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{|h|}{h}\) does not exist.
  5. Use a graphing tool such as Desmos to plot \(g(x)=|x|\text{,}\) and zoom in on the point where \(x = 0\text{.}\) Explain why it makes sense that \(g'(0)\) does not exist.
